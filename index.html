Why this reform now

Post–Hurricane Melissa operating reality

  • Volatility highlighted predictable coordination pressure points across functions and agencies.
  • MIIC must absorb and deploy recovery support under fiscal pressure and continuity urgency.
  • This reform strengthens delivery discipline without changing the mission.

Scale of the shock

  • Estimated damages: US$8.8B
  • Recovery package pledged: US$6.7B (including institutional strengthening support)
  • Implication: governance must support speed, coordination, and accountable execution.

What success looks like

Faster decisions

  • Routine decisions delegated with clear limits.
  • Escalation paths are predictable and time-bound.
  • Emergency pre-delegations activate under shock conditions.

Visible delivery

  • Monthly ministry and agency scorecards.
  • Corrective action plans for off-track KPIs.
  • Quarterly deep dives on outcomes (not just activity).

Resilience and continuity

  • Critical services meet uptime targets.
  • Recovery time meets RTO for critical functions.
  • Regular BCM drills and lessons learned.
Targets are finalised from baselines and ambition levels during implementation.

Where the Minister fits

Clear lines protect good governance and keep delivery moving

Minister (political executive)

  • Sets strategic direction and policy priorities for the portfolio.
  • Approves major policy positions, sensitive matters, and submissions that require political sign-off.
  • Receives a concise performance brief (monthly) and outcome deep dives (quarterly).

Permanent Secretary (administrative head)

  • Leads implementation, management discipline, and day-to-day execution.
  • Owns the governance cadence, delegations, and delivery routines.
  • Ensures agencies are unblocked, supported, and held to visible performance commitments.
This framework strengthens clarity and throughput. It is not a critique of the current team’s intent or effort.

The New Governance Spine

A simple overlay that makes decision-making faster, delivery visible, and continuity real
Decision Rights

RACI/RAPID mapping for policy, operations, procurement, crisis decisions, and digital/AI approvals.

Lean Committees

Small set of specialised committees with TORs, dashboards, decision authority, and escalation rules.

Delivery Unit

Monthly scorecards, action logs, corrective action follow-through, and cross-portfolio unblocking.

ISO-Aligned Controls

Practical management system: ISO 9001 (quality) + ISO 22301 (continuity) + risk discipline.

Crisis Readiness

Rapid-response mode, pre-delegations, continuity targets, drills, and after-action improvements.

Digital & AI Governance

Data owners, dashboard routines, safe AI use-cases, privacy/ethics controls, and procurement guardrails.

Cluster Delivery

Agencies execute closer to the frontline, with accountability contracts, scorecards, and escalation.

Ministerial oversight: Minister sets direction and signs off on high-sensitivity or major policy items. The PS runs the operating cadence and delivery routines within that direction.
This spine works in “normal mode” and tightens automatically in “crisis mode”.

What changes, practically

From

  • Authority and escalation that can be unclear across workflows.
  • Meetings that do not always convert into decisions and action logs.
  • Reporting that is fragmented and difficult to compare across agencies.
  • Continuity planning that needs regular testing and reinforcement.

To

  • Decision rights mapped per major process and used consistently.
  • Lean governance committees with TORs, cadence, and decision outputs.
  • Delivery Unit compiles scorecards, flags issues, and drives follow-through.
  • ISO-aligned documentation, drills, and embedded risk routines.

Decision rights and escalation

Decision clarity tools

  • RACI: Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed for each major process.
  • RAPID: Recommend, Agree, Perform, Input, Decide for high-stakes decisions.
  • Routine approvals delegated downward with controls.
  • Pre-delegations defined for emergencies so action does not stall.
  • Minister boundary: policy direction, sensitive matters, and major submissions remain clearly identified for Ministerial sign-off.

Immediate quick win (Month 1)

  • PS issues a decision-rights clarification memo (interim delegations and protocols).
  • Where relevant, the Minister endorses the direction so the operating signal is unambiguous.
  • Creates fast relief from minor bottlenecks without waiting on perfect systems.

Cadence that creates execution

Weekly

  • Reform Steering Team troubleshooting and unblocking.
  • Short, decision-oriented agenda.

Monthly

  • Performance and Delivery Committee reviews scorecards.
  • Deep dive on red KPIs and corrective action plans.
  • Delivery Unit circulates scorecards ahead of meeting.

Quarterly

  • Strategy Committee deep dive on outcomes and constraints.
  • Minister receives a focused outcomes brief to support priority decisions and external reporting.

Performance and Delivery System

How it works

  • Delivery Unit compiles ministry and agency scorecards from the KPI library.
  • Consistency checks, discrepancy flags, and RAG status applied.
  • Monthly review follows a disciplined agenda: outcomes first, then off-track KPIs, then corrective actions.
  • Corrective action plans are owned, dated, and followed up.
This creates transparency and makes delivery visible without adding noise.

Minister touchpoint

  • Monthly: short dashboard summary for awareness and alignment.
  • Quarterly: outcome deep dive for priority decisions and external commitments.
  • Escalation: issues that require political direction are clearly flagged, not blended into routine ops.

Crisis readiness and continuity (ISO 22301)

What is implemented

  • Business continuity policy, impact analysis, continuity plans for critical activities.
  • Clear crisis roles and responsibilities; drills and exercises to validate plans.
  • Committee oversight that turns plans into routine practice.
  • Option to target external audit or certification by 2026 to lock in discipline.

Minister in crisis mode

  • Sets political direction on sensitive matters and public messaging.
  • Receives rapid updates on critical services, high-impact risks, and public-facing actions.
  • PS runs execution through pre-delegations and continuity routines.

Digital and AI enablement

Digital service migration

  • Track % of services offered online and usage rates.
  • Use quarterly reviews to keep major IT projects on track.

Data-driven management

  • Dashboards become standard input to management decisions.
  • Delivery Unit supports data consistency and credibility.

Approval clarity

  • PS approves operational delivery and governance routines.
  • Minister sign-off is clearly defined for high-sensitivity items and major submissions.

Capacity and people plan

Small state approach

  • Multi-skilling: secondary skills that increase agility during crises.
  • Work stays interesting and can support retention.
  • Targeted skill injection where capacity is thin.

How capacity is measured

  • Turnover rate, critical vacancies filled, training hours per employee.
  • Staff survey indicators (workload and morale).
  • % of reform action items delivered on time (capacity sufficiency signal).

Implementation roadmap (18 months)

Phase 1 (0–90 days)

  • Form Reform Steering Team, meet weekly.
  • Emergency continuity fixes and stabilisation.
  • Decision-rights clarification memo (Month 1).
  • Performance monitoring (lite version) begins in Month 1.

Phase 2 (3–9 months)

  • Build the full KPI library and data routines.
  • Formalise committee TORs, templates, and reporting packs.
  • Training and workshops to embed new behaviours.
  • Initial IT and digital upgrades to support dashboards.

Phase 3 (9–18 months)

  • ISO maturity and continuous improvement routines.
  • Scale successful digital and AI pilots.
  • Institutionalise practices so they are not person-dependent.
  • Optional ISO audits (Quality and Continuity) to lock in discipline.
Minister receives quarterly outcome reviews to support portfolio prioritisation and external commitments.

Key risks and mitigations

Coordination breakdown

  • Risk: agencies drift back into silos under pressure.
  • Mitigation: clear roles, buy-in, performance commitments, early intervention.
  • Early warning: missing data, repeated delays, unresolved blockers.

Reform fatigue

  • Risk: overload slows adoption.
  • Mitigation: pace via roadmap, quick wins, clear comms, champions.
  • Early warning: morale drop, training participation falls, rising churn.

Data limitations

  • Risk: weak data undermines confidence in scorecards.
  • Mitigation: early focus on governance, cleaning, and standardised collection.
  • Early warning: inconsistent metrics and repeated discrepancies.
